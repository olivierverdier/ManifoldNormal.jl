ManifoldNormal.scaled_distance ‚Äî Method
scaled_distance(D::ProjLogNormal, x)

Start with a ProjLogNormal(Œº,Œ£) distribution with mean $Œº$ lying on the sample manifold $M$, and covariance $Œ£$ in the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ of the group $G$. The infinitesimal action of the group $G$ on the manifold $M$ gives rise to the linear map $P \colon \mathfrak{g} \to T_{Œº}M$, which in turns gives the projected covariance $PŒ£P^*$ on the tangent space $T_{Œº}M$.

One then measures the scaled distance from $Œº$ to $x$ from $v := \log(Œº, x)$ with the formula

\[\frac{\sqrt{v^T (PŒ£P^*)^{-1} v}}{\sqrt{n}}\]

(where $v$ is regarded as a column matrix here) and $n$ is the dimension of the sample manifold $M$.
