ManifoldNormal.jl

This packages defines distributions and noises on manifolds. They are defined as follows:

  • A distribution is a fully prescribed probability distribution on a given manifold; an example is the ActionDistribution distribution.
  • A noise is a distribution on a manifold, but parameterized by a point on that manifold; examples are IsotropicNoise, ActionNoise and NoNoise.

Noise Models

A general interface for noises on manifolds.

Action Noise

Consider a group action $G ‚äÇ \mathrm{Diff}(M)$. The push-forward of a normal distribution on a Lie algebra. The push-forward at a point $x\in M$ is done by the function $F : \mathfrak{g} \to M$ defined by

\[F(Œæ) = \exp(Œæ)‚ãÖ x\]

Isotropic Noise

This noise at a point $x$ on a metric manifold $M$ is the push-forward by the metric exponential of a centred normal distribution on the tangent space $T_x M$. The covariance of that normal distribution is equal to the metric at that point $x$.

ManifoldNormal.scaled_distance ‚Äî Method
scaled_distance(D::ActionDistribution, x)

Start with a ActionDistribution(Œº,Œ£) distribution with mean $Œº$ lying on the sample manifold $M$, and covariance $Œ£$ in the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ of the group $G$. The infinitesimal action of the group $G$ on the manifold $M$ gives rise to the linear map $P \colon \mathfrak{g} \to T_{Œº}M$, which in turns gives the projected covariance $PŒ£P^*$ on the tangent space $T_{Œº}M$.

One then measures the scaled distance from $Œº$ to $x$ from $v := \log(Œº, x)$ with the formula

\[\frac{\sqrt{v^T (PŒ£P^*)^{-1} v}}{\sqrt{n}}\]

(where $v$ is regarded as a column matrix here) and $n$ is the dimension of the sample manifold $M$.
